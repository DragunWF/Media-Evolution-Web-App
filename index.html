Evolution of Media in the Philippines

Performance Task #2

Marc Plarisan | Grade 12 ABM-C

Pre-Colonial Period

This time period was back when filipinos were just tribal people and our literature was mainly based on oral traditions uninfluenced by outsiders.

Spanish Period

This time period was around the time the spanish arrived at the Philippine islands. Christianity, theatrical forms, and Spanish traditions were introduced to our people.

American Period

This time period is around the time when the Americans pushed the Spanish out of the Philippines. This was the time when radios, american influence on clothing, and food came to a rise on the filipino people.

Japanese Period

This time period was around World War 2 when the Japanese occupied the Philippines. This was a dark time that took years for the Philippines to recover.

Martial Law Period

This period was around Ferdinand Marcos's reign. The country went through a lot of censorship and hardships. The media during this time period was fully censored.

Post-Martial Law Period

This period around the end of Marcos's dictatorship. Censorship was lifted and the freedom of speech and expression were reinstated. This was also around the time when the internet was first made available to the Philippines.

Question

"If you were given the chance to live in one of the periods in the evolution of the media in the Philippines, which period would you like to experience living in? Why?"

Personally, I would definitely like to experience living in the post-martial law period. In that time period, there's quite a lot of opportunity to be taken especially since it is during the time when the internet was first made available to our country.

In addition to my previous reason, compared to the other time periods; The post-martial law period is also when our country is at the most peaceful state. So of course, I'd rather live in this time period when our risk of death is at its lowest.
